    About Island Clovers

    At Island Clovers, we are driven by a passion for sustainable agriculture and the well-being of the vegan community. We recognized the challenge of obtaining sufficient vitamin B12 on a plant-based diet and embarked on a mission to develop a natural, effective solution.

    Our team is dedicated to pioneering innovative methods in agricultural technology, focusing on the cultivation of nutrient-rich duckweed. We believe in the power of nature and the potential of symbiotic relationships between plants and microorganisms to enhance nutritional value.

    We are committed to transparency, ethical practices, and providing high-quality, naturally enriched B12 to support a healthy and vibrant vegan lifestyle.

    Our Innovative Duckweed System

    Our core innovation lies in our proprietary Duckweed–Bacterial Culture (DBC) system. We co-culture edible duckweed (Lemnaceae) with carefully selected vitamin B12–producing bacteria, such as [P. freudenreichii] and [Lactobacillus spp.]. Scientific research has demonstrated that duckweed can accumulate significant levels of bioactive B12 only in the presence of these beneficial bacteria [Acosta et al. 2024].

    Key Features of Our System:

    • Integrated Aquatic Bioreactor: Our system utilizes a controlled environment, such as a pond or tank, designed to foster a stable coexistence between duckweed and a bacterial biofilm.
    • Tailored Nutrient Medium: We provide a precisely balanced nutrient solution containing essential macro- and micronutrients, including cobalt, a crucial precursor for B12 synthesis.
    • Optimized Conditions: We maintain a controlled pH level (around 6.5), along with adequate light and gentle aeration to support the health and productivity of both the duckweed and the bacteria.
    • Natural B12 Production: Our chosen bacteria, including the food-grade [P. freudenreichii], naturally convert trace cobalt and organic substrates into cobalamin (vitamin B12), which is then incorporated into the duckweed biomass.

    This bioaugmented aquaculture approach allows the duckweed to efficiently fix carbon and nutrients, while the co-cultured bacteria enrich the harvested biomass with natural vitamin B12, offering a sustainable and plant-based source of this essential nutrient.

    Our Commitment to Research & Development

    Island Clovers is founded on a strong scientific basis and a commitment to continuous improvement through rigorous research and development.

    Research Background:

    Plants do not naturally produce vitamin B12; it is synthesized only by certain bacteria and archaea. However, groundbreaking research has identified bioactive B12 in duckweed varieties like Wolffia and Lemna [Acosta et al. 2024], [Weissberger et al. 2020]. Studies have shown that this B12 is produced by associated bacteria, either living within the plant tissues (endophytic) or forming a biofilm on the surface [Acosta et al. 2024].

    Our approach leverages these findings by intentionally co-cultivating selected, safe, and high-B12-producing bacteria with duckweed. We are constantly exploring and optimizing our system, including:

    • Screening for higher-yielding bacterial strains.
    • Refining our nutrient medium for optimal B12 enrichment.
    • Optimizing our bioreactor design for scalability and efficiency.
    • Investigating the effects of different light and aeration conditions.
    • Exploring potential co-products from duckweed biomass.

    We are dedicated to staying at the forefront of scientific advancements in this field to provide the most effective and sustainable natural B12 source for the vegan community.

    Regulatory Approval and Safety

    At Island Clovers, safety and compliance are paramount. We are committed to adhering to all relevant regulations to ensure the quality and safety of our B12-enriched duckweed system.

    We prioritize the use of food-grade bacterial strains with established safety profiles. For instance, [Propionibacterium freudenreichii] has GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) status in the U.S. and QPS (Qualified Presumption of Safety) status in Europe, indicating its safety for consumption.

    We are actively investigating and pursuing the necessary regulatory approvals for our final product, which may include GRAS self-affirmation or food additive petitions with the FDA, depending on the intended market (human food or animal feed). We are also mindful of international regulations and will strive for compliance in relevant markets.

    Our commitment includes rigorous testing and quality assurance protocols to ensure our product meets the highest safety standards for the vegan community.

    Market Trials and Future Plans

    We are currently in the pilot phase of our project, conducting small-scale trials to validate the effectiveness of our B12 enrichment process. These trials involve carefully controlled experiments to measure B12 content and optimize our cultivation methods.

    Our next steps include scaling up our operation to a pilot scale (100–1000 L) to further refine our processes and gather data on larger-scale production. We plan to engage with potential pilot customers, such as organic animal feed producers and vegan supplement companies, to test our product and gather valuable feedback.

    We are also exploring different product forms, such as fresh frozen mash and dried powder, and will assess how processing affects B12 retention. Our ultimate goal is to demonstrate a consistent and scalable process for producing naturally enriched B12 duckweed, paving the way for full-scale commercialization and partnerships to bring this sustainable nutrient source to the vegan community.
